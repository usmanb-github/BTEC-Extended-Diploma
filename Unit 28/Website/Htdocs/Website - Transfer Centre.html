Big Clubs will laugh at £25 million Reus fee - Kahn

The 25-year-old had the stipulation inserted in his existing deal - which runs until June 2017 - upon signing for BVB from Borussia Monchengladbach in 2012.Bayern Munich, Real Madrid and Manchester City are all rumoured to be interested in the player, while Reus himself has shown little interest in extending his contract.
BVB have admitted they will be powerless in the event he decides to depart and ex-Bayern keeper Kahn expects him to do exactly that. "It will be very difficult for Dortmund to hold on to Reus, especially if we take into account that they are unlikely to qualify for European football,” the ex-Bayern goalkeeper told Sport Bild.
"Dortmund will only be able to hold on to Reus if they push their own financial limits. If not, Reus will definitely walk out.
"The big clubs in Europe laugh about a €25m transfer fee these days…"

BREAKING: Arteta signs new Arsenal deal

Mikel Arteta has signed a one-year contract extension with Arsenal, according to his agent. Arteta's representative Inaki Ibanez told footmercato.net: "Mikel is no longer free next June, because he now has an extra year on his contract." This means Arteta will stay at the Emirates until the end of 2015-16.

Gabriel Paulista could make his debut for Arsenal

Gabriel Paulista will fast tracked into the Arsenal first team following his arrival from Villarreal and could make his debut on Sunday against Aston Villa. Arsenal have yet to annouce his purchase, but rumours have spread that Paulista could make his debut as soon as this Sunday.

'Pogba will never go back to Manchester United' - Pirlo

Pogba left United for the Serie A champions in July 2012 after growing frustrated with a lack of first-team opportunities under Sir Alex Ferguson.

The 21-year-old immediately became a regular fixture at Juventus and has already won two Serie A titles during his time in Italy.

"For whatever reasons they [Manchester United] chose not to give him a chance, and that is a mistake they will have to live with now," Pirlo told Telefoot.

"I can’t see that he [Pogba] will ever return to Manchester United."
